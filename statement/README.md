### Summary

  This paper compares two approaches to building smaller language models:
  training from scratch versus pruning larger pretrained models. Under
  matched training-token budgets, pruned models consistently outperform
  randomly initialized models. The advantage narrows as training budgets
  increase and as pruning becomes more aggressive, suggesting that pruning
  provides a useful initialization rather than an inherent guarantee of
  superior performance.
